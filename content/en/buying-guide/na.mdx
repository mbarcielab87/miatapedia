# NA (1989-1997) Buying Guide

*Your comprehensive guide to finding the perfect first-generation MX-5*

The NA MX-5 is an iconic sports car that offers pure driving pleasure at an affordable price. However, these 25-35 year old cars require careful inspection to avoid costly surprises. This guide covers everything you need to know before buying your first (or next) NA.

## Quick Overview

**Price Range (2024):** $8,000 - $25,000+
**Sweet Spot:** $12,000 - $18,000 for good condition
**Best Value:** 1994-1997 NA8 models
**Avoid:** High-mileage automatic transmissions, heavily modified examples

## NA6 vs NA8: Which to Choose?

### NA6 (1989-1993)
**Pros:**
- Lighter weight (940kg) - better power-to-weight ratio
- More connected driving feel
- Classic pop-up headlights
- Generally cheaper to buy

**Cons:**
- Less power (115hp)
- Short nose crank issues (1989-1991)
- Fewer safety features
- Basic interior

### NA8 (1994-1997)
**Pros:**
- More power (130hp) and torque
- Improved interior and ergonomics
- Better safety features (dual airbags from 1994)
- More refined driving experience

**Cons:**
- Slightly heavier (1040kg)
- Usually more expensive
- Less raw driving feel

**Recommendation:** For most buyers, a 1994-1997 NA8 offers the best balance of performance, refinement, and reliability.

## Critical Inspection Points

### 1. Rust (Most Important!)
NA MX-5s are prone to rust in specific areas. Check carefully:

**Common Rust Spots:**
- **Rear wheel arches** - Inside and outside
- **Sills/door frames** - Along the bottom edge
- **Front crossmember** - Structural component
- **Rear subframe** - Where suspension mounts
- **Door frames** - Around window seals
- **Battery tray** - Under hood, driver's side

**Red Flags:**
- Bubbling paint over sills
- Soft spots when pressed
- Recent paint touch-ups hiding rust
- Welded patches (unless professionally done)

### 2. Engine Health

**NA6 Short Nose Crank (1989-1991):**
- Check if timing belt service included crankshaft inspection
- Listen for rough idle or metallic noises
- Avoid 1989-1991 NA6s unless crank has been verified/replaced

**General Engine Checks:**
- Oil leaks around valve cover gasket
- Coolant leaks at radiator/water pump
- Blue smoke from exhaust (worn rings)
- Check compression if possible (should be 180+ psi)

### 3. Transmission

**Manual Transmission:**
- Test all gears, including reverse
- Check for grinding into 2nd gear (common wear point)
- Feel for smooth clutch engagement
- Look for clutch fluid leaks

**Automatic (Avoid if possible):**
- Rough shifting indicates expensive problems
- Check for delayed engagement
- Look for fluid leaks (expensive to fix)

### 4. Suspension & Handling

**Check for:**
- Worn shock absorbers (bouncy ride)
- Ball joint wear (clunking noises)
- Tire wear patterns indicating alignment issues
- Power steering leaks (1991+ models)

### 5. Electrical Systems

**Pop-up Headlights:**
- Test both up/down operation
- Check for slow or uneven operation
- Listen for grinding motor sounds
- Verify manual override works

**Other Electrical:**
- All lights and indicators
- Gauges and warning lights
- Air conditioning (if equipped)
- Radio and electrical accessories

## Mileage Considerations

**Low Mileage (Under 60k miles):**
- Premium pricing expected
- Check for garage queen issues (dried seals, old fluids)
- Verify maintenance records

**Average Mileage (60k-120k miles):**
- Sweet spot for most buyers
- Should have maintenance history
- Timing belt service critical at this range

**High Mileage (120k+ miles):**
- Can be good value if well-maintained
- Expect more frequent repairs
- Critical to verify maintenance history

## What to Pay

### Market Segments (2024)

**Project Cars ($8,000-$12,000):**
- High mileage or needs work
- Rust issues requiring repair
- Good for experienced mechanics

**Good Drivers ($12,000-$18,000):**
- Clean examples with minor issues
- Maintenance history available
- Ready to drive and enjoy

**Excellent Examples ($18,000-$25,000):**
- Low mileage, exceptional condition
- Complete maintenance records
- Potentially appreciating assets

**Collector Grade ($25,000+):**
- Museum quality, very low miles
- Original, unmodified examples
- Investment grade vehicles

## Modifications to Consider

**Good Modifications:**
- Timing belt service with water pump
- Upgraded cooling system
- Performance tires and alignment
- Maintenance and reliability upgrades

**Red Flag Modifications:**
- Engine swaps (unless professionally done)
- Extensive chassis modifications
- Poor quality aftermarket parts
- "Stance" modifications affecting drivability

## Final Purchase Checklist

**Before Buying:**
- [ ] Professional inspection if possible
- [ ] Verify clean title
- [ ] Check maintenance records
- [ ] Test drive in various conditions
- [ ] Budget for immediate needs ($2,000-$5,000)

**First 30 Days After Purchase:**
- [ ] Full fluid change (engine, transmission, differential)
- [ ] Timing belt service if overdue
- [ ] Brake fluid flush
- [ ] Comprehensive safety inspection

## Common Buyer Mistakes

1. **Focusing only on price** - A cheap car with rust will cost more long-term
2. **Ignoring maintenance records** - Unknown history = future problems
3. **Buying the first example** - Shop around, good ones exist
4. **Underestimating ongoing costs** - Budget for maintenance
5. **Buying someone else's project** - Avoid heavily modified examples

## Conclusion

The NA MX-5 offers one of the purest driving experiences available, but buying the right example is crucial. Take your time, inspect thoroughly, and don't be afraid to walk away from questionable examples. A good NA will provide years of driving pleasure and potentially appreciate in value.

Remember: it's better to pay more for a well-maintained example than to save money on a project that will cost more in the long run.
